Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

1630 - 1680

Father Of Maratha Empire

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, born in 1630 in Shivneri Fort near Pune, Maharashtra, India, was a legendary warrior king and the founder of the Maratha Empire. His life and accomplishments are deeply intertwined with the history of medieval India, particularly during a time of political turmoil and foreign invasions.

Shivaji Maharaj was born to Shahaji Bhonsle, a prominent Maratha general in the service of the Sultan of Bijapur, and Jijabai, a devout and influential woman known for her piety and courage. From a young age, Shivaji displayed leadership qualities and a keen interest in military affairs. He was deeply influenced by his mother's teachings and the valorous tales of heroes like Maharana Pratap and the Vijayanagara Emperor, Krishnadevaraya.

Rise to Power

Shivaji Maharaj began his military career by capturing Torna Fort in 1646 at the age of 16, marking the start of his efforts to establish an independent Maratha kingdom. Over the next few decades, he gradually expanded his territory by capturing forts and strongholds in the Sahyadri mountain ranges and along the Konkan coast. His military campaigns were marked by innovative guerrilla tactics, swift mobility, and strategic alliances with local chiefs and nobles.

Naval and Military Tactics

Recognizing the strategic importance of naval power, Shivaji Maharaj built a formidable navy and successfully challenged European naval powers like the Portuguese and the British. His naval achievements secured Maratha control over key coastal regions and facilitated trade and communication across his empire.

Administration and Governance

Shivaji Maharaj was not only a skilled warrior but also a visionary administrator. He established a well-organized administrative system with decentralized governance. He introduced systems for revenue collection, maintained a well-equipped navy, and promoted religious tolerance and justice within his kingdom. Shivaji Maharaj's administrative reforms laid the foundation for the Maratha Empire's expansion and stability.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Administration

  • Peshwas the most important minister who looked after the finance and general administration.
  • Senapati (sari-i-naubat) was one of the leading Marathas chiefs who were basically posted of honour.
  • The Majumdar was an accountant.
  • The Wakenavis is one who looks after the intelligence, post and household affairs.
  • The Surnavis or chitnis assist the king with his correspondence.
  • The Dabir was the master of ceremonies and helps the king in his dealing with foreign affairs.
  • The Nayayadish and Punditrao were in charge of justice and charitable grants.
  • He levies the tax on the land which was one-fourth of the land revenue i.e. Chauth or Chauthai.
  • He was not only proved to be an able general, a skilful tactician, and a shrewd diplomat, he also laid the foundation of a strong state by curbing the power of the Deshmukh.
